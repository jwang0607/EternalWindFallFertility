Understanding Surrogacy

A compassionate approach to helping families grow through gestational surrogacy

What is Gestational Surrogacy?

Gestational surrogacy is a reproductive arrangement where a woman carries and delivers a baby for another person or couple. The surrogate is not genetically related to the child, as the embryo is created using the intended parents' or donors' genetic material through IVF.

Who Chooses Surrogacy?

Surrogacy may be right for couples who cannot carry a pregnancy, single individuals wanting to become parents, or same-sex couples looking to build their families. Our compassionate approach ensures each journey is personalized to your needs.
