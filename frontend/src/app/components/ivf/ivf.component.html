What is IVF?

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is an assisted reproductive technology where an egg is combined with sperm outside the body. The fertilized egg (embryo) is then transferred to the uterus. IVF is a key treatment for infertility and is the most effective form of assisted reproductive technology.

IVF with Donor Egg

Creating families through donor egg IVF

Donor egg IVF is an assisted reproductive technology that uses eggs from a donor to help intended parents conceive. This process is particularly beneficial for women who cannot produce viable eggs due to advanced maternal age, premature ovarian failure, genetic concerns, or other medical conditions.

Donor Selection

We provide access to a diverse pool of carefully screened egg donors, allowing you to select a donor based on physical characteristics, educational background, and other important factors.

Success Rates

Donor egg IVF typically has higher success rates than traditional IVF, as donor eggs come from young, healthy women with optimal reproductive health.

Frozen Embryo Transfer

Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET) allows you to use previously frozen embryos from a prior IVF cycle, providing a less invasive and often more successful approach to achieving pregnancy. Our advanced freezing techniques ensure excellent embryo survival rates.

Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET)

Utilizing cryopreserved embryos for future pregnancy

Frozen Embryo Transfer (FET) is a procedure where embryos from a previous IVF cycle that were cryopreserved (frozen) are thawed and transferred to the uterus. This approach allows for multiple chances at pregnancy from a single egg retrieval and can be more cost-effective and less physically demanding than repeating full IVF cycles.

Advanced Vitrification

We use state-of-the-art vitrification techniques that significantly improve embryo survival rates after thawing compared to older freezing methods.

Better Timing

FET allows for optimal timing of embryo transfer, as it can be scheduled when the uterine lining is most receptive, potentially increasing success rates.

Preimplantation Genetic Screening

PGS helps identify chromosomally normal embryos before transfer, significantly increasing IVF success rates and reducing the risk of miscarriage. This advanced technology is especially beneficial for patients with recurrent pregnancy loss or previous IVF failures.

Preimplantation Genetic Screening (PGS)

Selecting healthy embryos through genetic testing

Preimplantation Genetic Screening (PGS), also known as Preimplantation Genetic Testing for Aneuploidy (PGT-A), is a technique used to examine embryos for chromosomal abnormalities before transfer during an IVF cycle. This screening helps identify embryos with the correct number of chromosomes, increasing the chances of a successful pregnancy and reducing the risk of miscarriage.

Comprehensive Screening

Our PGS technology screens all 23 pairs of chromosomes, providing a complete genetic profile of each embryo.

Improved Outcomes

PGS can significantly reduce the risk of miscarriage and increase live birth rates by ensuring only chromosomally normal embryos are transferred.

Our state-of-the-art fertility preservation services allow you to freeze eggs or sperm for future family building. This option is ideal for those facing medical treatments that may affect fertility, those wanting to delay parenthood, or those looking to preserve their fertility while still young.

Egg And Sperm Banking

Preserving fertility for future family building

Egg and sperm banking, also known as gamete cryopreservation, allows individuals to freeze and store their reproductive cells for future use. This service is particularly valuable for those facing medical treatments that may affect fertility, those who wish to delay parenthood, or those who want to preserve their fertility while they are still young.

Fertility Preservation

Banking your eggs or sperm can give you the flexibility to build your family on your own timeline, preserving your fertility at its peak.

Medical Protection

For patients facing cancer treatment or other medical procedures that may affect fertility, gamete banking offers a way to protect future family-building options.

The IVF Process

1

Ovarian Stimulation

Medications are used to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple eggs instead of the single egg that normally develops each month.

2

Egg Retrieval

Once the eggs have matured, they are retrieved through a minor surgical procedure under light sedation.

3

Fertilization

The retrieved eggs are combined with sperm in a laboratory dish to allow fertilization to occur.

4

Embryo Development

The fertilized eggs develop into embryos over 3-5 days and are closely monitored by our embryologists.

5

Embryo Transfer

The healthiest embryo(s) are transferred to the uterus in a simple procedure that doesn't require anesthesia.

6

Pregnancy Test

A blood test is performed about two weeks after the embryo transfer to determine if pregnancy has been achieved.

Advanced IVF Technologies

ICSI

Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI) involves injecting a single sperm directly into an egg to facilitate fertilization.

PGT

Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT) screens embryos for genetic abnormalities before they are transferred to the uterus.

Embryo Freezing

Cryopreservation of embryos allows for storage of extra embryos for future use, increasing the efficiency of IVF.
