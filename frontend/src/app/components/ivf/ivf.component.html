What is IVF?

In Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is an assisted reproductive technology where an egg is combined with sperm outside the body. The fertilized egg (embryo) is then transferred to the uterus. IVF is a key treatment for infertility and is the most effective form of assisted reproductive technology.

The IVF Process

1

Ovarian Stimulation

Medications are used to stimulate the ovaries to produce multiple eggs instead of the single egg that normally develops each month.

2

Egg Retrieval

Once the eggs have matured, they are retrieved through a minor surgical procedure under light sedation.

3

Fertilization

The retrieved eggs are combined with sperm in a laboratory dish to allow fertilization to occur.

4

Embryo Development

The fertilized eggs develop into embryos over 3-5 days and are closely monitored by our embryologists.

5

Embryo Transfer

The healthiest embryo(s) are transferred to the uterus in a simple procedure that doesn't require anesthesia.

6

Pregnancy Test

A blood test is performed about two weeks after the embryo transfer to determine if pregnancy has been achieved.

Advanced IVF Technologies

ICSI

Intracytoplasmic Sperm Injection (ICSI) involves injecting a single sperm directly into an egg to facilitate fertilization.

PGT

Preimplantation Genetic Testing (PGT) screens embryos for genetic abnormalities before they are transferred to the uterus.

Embryo Freezing

Cryopreservation of embryos allows for storage of extra embryos for future use, increasing the efficiency of IVF.

Donor Services

We offer egg, sperm, and embryo donation services for those who need assistance from third-party reproduction.
